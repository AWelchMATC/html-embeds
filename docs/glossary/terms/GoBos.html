GoBo's

Tags: Lighting, Cinematography, shaders, materials, Texture

Gobo lighting sounds fancy. It stands for "GOes Before Optics." Think of it as a stencil for light. You put a gobo (a metal, glass, or plastic cut-out) in front of a light source, and voila! You project patterns, shapes, or even images.


Gobos are used with projectors and simpler light sources to create lighting scenes in theatrical applications. Simple gobos, incorporated into automated lighting systems, are popular at nightclubs and other musical venues to create moving shapes.
