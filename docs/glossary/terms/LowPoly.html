Low Poly Mesh

Tags: Modeling, Topology, Baking

Definition Overview:

The term “Low Poly” refers to the Game Res Mesh (also sometimes called the Render Mesh or Arbitrary Mesh). This is a mesh that is intentionally low in polygon density, with no unnecessary faces, vertices, or edges.

This is the mesh used in‑game. Its topology is determined by real‑time constraints rather than subdivision modeling rules. Every polygon exists to support silhouette, shading, UVs, or gameplay data — excess geometry wastes memory and negatively impacts performance.

Low poly meshes typically consist of triangles to ensure predictable performance, and are not the same mesh used to create a subdivided or sculpted high poly.

A related term is “Mid‑Poly”. While somewhat subjective, the term is commonly used to describe game‑resolution meshes that do not rely on normal‑map baking from a high poly, instead using additional geometry with tiling textures or trim sheets.
